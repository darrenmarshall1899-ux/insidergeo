Romania Is Building the EU's Largest Gas Project — And NATO Has a Grey Zone It Can't Ignore

GeoInsider · Jun 18, 2026 · Originally published on X / @InsiderGeo

Romania Is Building the EU's Largest Gas Project in the Black Sea and NATO has a grey zone it can't ignore 🧵👇

1/ Romania is building the EU's largest gas project in the Black Sea. 160km offshore, construction is already underway. By 2027, it could double Romania's gas production and help reduce Europe's dependence on Russian gas.

2/ The project is called Neptun Deep.
It consists of two offshore gas fields — Domino and Pelican South — discovered in 2012 when Romania drilled its first deepwater well in the Black Sea.
Developed by OMV Petrom and Romgaz, it represents a €4 billion investment.

3/ The numbers:
🔹 ~100 bcm recoverable gas 🔹 10 production wells 🔹 1 offshore platform 🔹 160km pipeline to shore 🔹 ~8 bcm/year at peak production
For comparison, Romania currently produces around 8-9 bcm annually.

4/ Construction has been underway since the final investment decision was approved in 2023 with drilling operations beginning in 2025 and pipeline installation starting in 2026. Neptun Deep is currently the largest offshore natural gas project under development anywhere in the EU.

5/ First gas is expected in 2027.
Germany, Moldova and Slovakia have already shown interest in future supplies.
For Europe, every new source of gas means less dependence on Russia.

6/ The problem?
Neptun Deep sits inside Romania's Exclusive Economic Zone (EEZ), around 160km from the coast. Not on Romanian territory. Not in Romanian territorial waters.
And that distinction sits at the heart of the debate surrounding the project.

7/ NATO's Article 5 covers allied territory and territorial waters.
Neptun Deep sits 160km offshore inside Romania's EEZ — a different legal zone.
That's why attacks or sabotage against the project raise questions.

8/ Romania's Chief of Defence, General Gheorghiță Vlad, publicly raised concerns about this issue in early 2026.
Speaking about Neptun Deep, he warned that Romania's EEZ is not covered by NATO's Article 5 and that interference in the area remains a possibility.

9/ He warned that the Black Sea security environment has changed significantly since Russia's invasion of Ukraine in 2022. Russian ships and aircraft continue operating in the region, while concerns over critical infrastructure have steadily grown.

10/ According to the Romanian Chief of Defence:
🔹 More than 120 sea mines have been detected in the Black Sea since 2022 🔹 GPS interference is reported regularly
"Many dangers, created mainly by the Russian Federation, continue to exist."

11/ Romania is trying to modernize its military.
Through the EU's SAFE programme, Bucharest is expected to receive €16.68 billion — second only to Poland.
Patrol vessels, mine countermeasure assets and Scorpène submarines are planned. But for now, much of it remains on paper.

12/ Romania's defense buildup faces another challenge: politics.
The government collapsed in May 2026, leaving a caretaker administration with limited powers.
That has created uncertainty around SAFE funding, PNRR funds and future defense spending.

13/ Also, before 2014, Ukraine's maritime zones created a buffer between Romania and Russia in the Black Sea.
Russia's annexation of Crimea changed that.
Today, Romania's EEZ directly borders Russia's.

14/ Europe is pouring billions into offshore energy. Platforms, cables, wind farms, pipelines — most of it inside EEZs. NATO's Article 5 leaves gaps here. The EU's Article 42(7) is slightly more explicit but neither is clear enough.

15/ NATO's Article 5 covers allied territory and territorial waters up to 22km from shore.
Neptun Deep sits 160km offshore inside Romania's EEZ, a different legal zone. NATO has never clearly defined how it would respond to an attack on critical infrastructure in EEZ.

16/ Until NATO defines what it will and won't defend beyond territorial waters, Romania's biggest project — and every other critical offshore project in Europe — remains vulnerable.

One of the most overlooked security questions in Europe right now.
Every offshore project inside an EEZ — platforms, pipelines, cables, wind farms — is at risk of sabotage, check the thread 👇
