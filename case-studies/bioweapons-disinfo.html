Russian Bioweapons Propaganda: The Document They Altered

GeoInsider · Jun 13, 2025 · Originally published on X / @InsiderGeo

THREAD | Russian propaganda is circulating a map claiming a new US intelligence document "confirms" American bioweapons labs in Ukraine. The document is real. What it actually says is close to the opposite of the claim. Let me walk through it.

First, the concessions, because they matter. ODNI did release a declassified slide deck on June 12, under outgoing DNI Tulsi Gabbard. US-funded labs in Ukraine are real. Black & Veatch did build them. Trump did sign a gain of function executive order. None of that is in dispute.

What's in dispute is what those facts mean. And here the propaganda gives itself away in one detail.
The real ODNI slide on the Kharkiv veterinary institute is titled "Ongoing Target for Russian Disinformation."
The Rybar graphic changes that title to "Russian Misinformation, Other Potential Operations."
They swapped one word and invented an entire phrase. The document is about Russia targeting this lab with disinformation. They flipped it into the document endorsing that disinformation.

The slide's own text says the institute "probably houses at least some dangerous pathogens and almost certainly remains vulnerable to long-standing Russian information operations, seizure or damage."
That's a risk assessment about a Russian target.

The labs themselves trace to the Cooperative Threat Reduction program, running since the 1990s. Its job is the opposite of building weapons: secure and destroy Soviet-era pathogen stocks, employ former Soviet weapons scientists in civilian work, run disease surveillance.

This isn't new and it was never secret. The Pentagon published the numbers years ago: about $200M since 2005 supporting 46 Ukrainian public health and diagnostic labs, all Ukrainian owned. The facility lists were posted publicly by the State Department and the US embassy in Kyiv.

Russia took this exact claim to the Biological Weapons Convention in 2022. Eighty-nine states heard it. Russia ended up supported by eight countries: Belarus, China, Cuba, Iran, Nicaragua, Syria, Venezuela, Zimbabwe. The US and Ukraine were backed by more than five times as many.

So the method is simple. Take a real document, alter its title, strip its context and sell the labs exist as if it proved the labs are bioweapons sites.

The first claim was true. The second was rejected by almost everyone who looked at it. Don't let the map do your thinking.
